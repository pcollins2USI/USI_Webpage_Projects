The Basic Strokes of Tennis

Forehand
In this most common of shots, the player turns the body so that the shoulder of arm not holding the racket faces the net.   The player then swings the racket forward to meet the ball and follows through over their shoulder.
Backhand
In the backhand stroke the player turns the other shoulder to the net and swings across the body.
Grips
In a basic grip the player grabs the bottom of the racket in a manner similar to shaking hands.  Many players now employ a two-handed grip when hitting a backhand.
Lob
A high, sharpley arced return is called a lob.  This shot is frequently employed to force your opponent away from the net.  Because of its high trajectory, it can also be used to give a player more time to regain positioning.
Overhead Smash
Frequently a lob shot is returned with an overhead smash.  This shot is hit in a manner similar to a serve and is very powerful.
Drop Shot
The drop shot is a lightly hit, spinning return that drops softly over the net, forcing the opponent to approach the net. If the opponent is positioned deep in the court, the drop shot can be used to win the point.
Volley
When playing close to the net, the most common type of stroke is a volley, which is a return of the ball before it bounces.
Half Volley
When a player returns a low ball just as it bounces on the court, that shot is referred to as a half volley.
